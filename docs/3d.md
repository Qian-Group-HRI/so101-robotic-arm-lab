<h2>Printing the Parts</h2>

A variety of common FDM 3D printers are suitable for producing all parts of the **follower** and **leader** arms. Follow the steps below to get a reliable, repeatable print instead of a pile of warped plastic.

---

### Step 1 – Choose a Printer

The provided STL files are ready to print on many FDM machines.

**Recommended settings**

- **Material:** PLA+  
- **Nozzle / layer height:**  
  - 0.4 mm nozzle @ 0.2 mm layer height, **or**  
  - 0.6 mm nozzle @ 0.4 mm layer height  
- **Infill density:** ~15%  
- **Sample printers:** Prusa MINI+, UP Plus 2, Creality Ender 3, Bambu Lab A/P/X series

Other printers may work, but if your prints look terrible while ignoring these settings, that’s on you.

---

### Step 2 – Set Up the Printer

Before you waste 12 hours of print time, do this:

- **Calibrate & level the bed** using your printer’s official procedure.  
- **Clean the print surface** so it’s free of dust and grease. If you use water/alcohol, dry the bed completely.  
- If recommended for your printer, apply a **thin, even layer of glue stick** across the print area. No clumps.  
- **Load the PLA+ filament** using the printer’s instructions and extrude a bit to confirm smooth flow.  
- In your slicer, make sure the settings match the recommendations above as closely as possible.  
- Enable **supports “everywhere”**, but:
  - Ignore slopes steeper than **45°** to the horizontal.
  - **Do not** generate supports inside screw holes with horizontal axes.

---

### Step 3 – Check Printer Accuracy (Gauges)

Before committing to a full build, verify dimensional accuracy using the gauges.

In the `gauges/` folder you’ll find gauges for:

- A **standard 4×2 LEGO block**  
- An **STS3215 servo**

Print:

- If you have an STS3215 servo:  
  - `gauge_zero`  
  - `gauge_tight`
- If you have a LEGO block:  
  - `gauge_zero`  
  - `gauge_minus_0_1`

Then:

1. Test `gauge_zero` against the reference object (servo or LEGO).  
2. The fit should be snug but not insane – similar to the fit shown in the official tutorial.  
3. If the fit is off:
   - Tweak your slicer/printer settings (flow, horizontal expansion, etc.) and reprint, **or**
   - Open an issue describing your printer + settings + photos.

Only move on once the gauges behave.

---

### Step 5 – Remove Supports & Clean Up

Once the print finishes:

- Use a **putty knife or scraper** to carefully remove parts from the bed. Don’t gouge the surface.  
- Remove all support material, especially around:
  - joint interfaces  
  - screw holes  
  - encoder / motor mounting regions  
- Inspect each part for obvious defects (layer separation, warped bases) and reprint any part that looks suspicious. Bad parts now = wasted time later during assembly.
